About Me

I completed by Bachelors in Engineering ( Major: Computer Science) in 2013 from BMS College of Engineering in Bangalore,India. I finished as a medalist for the university with a 9.44/10 or 4.0/4.0 GPA and was among the top 3 students. During my final semester, I interned at NetApp Inc. and worked with the Array Products Group. I then went on to work Full-time at NetApp Inc. from 2013 to 2014 and worked on implementing the CIFS protocol on the ONTAP operating system. Since October 2014, I have been pursuing my master's at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). Thanks to the rigorous course, I have had a chance to improve both, my domain knowledge in artificial intelligence and data analysis as well as my engineering skills. During the program, I was also selected to as a Graduate Student Researcher on the CDLI project funded by UCLA and Oxford. As part of this assignment, I am working on a search engine for ancient simerian tablets and texts. My task also includes developing cursory features for and end to end management of the website for the project.
